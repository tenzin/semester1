Punakha

Punakha is well known for the majestic fortress which stands at the confluence of two rivers. It is also the old capital of Bhutan. Kings of Bhutan are formally crowned in Punakha as per age old tradition. Tourist attractions in Punakha are:

  1. Punakha Dzong:

    2. Punakha Dzong (fortress) is one of the oldest structures in Bhutan, build in the 15th century. It lies at the confluence of two rivers, Pochu and Mochu.

    Traditional Mask Dance in Punakha (Image sourced from wikimedia)

    Punakha Dzong hosts the annual Punakha Domchoe, a three day traditional festival which exhibits traditional dance performance and religious enactments.

  2. River Rafting:

    3. The rivers flowing through Punakha valley is ideal for river rafting. It is one of the main tourist attraction in Punakha.

    River Rafting in Punakha (© Sithar Norbu, 2017. Reproduced with expressed permission.)

    There are licensed tour operators who provide rafting services. They provide tour packages include rafting gear, professional guide and other accessories and support.
