The kingdom of Bhutan is located in the Himalayas between India and China. It has a total area of 38,394 square kilometers.

Bhutan lays strong emphasis on preservation and promotion of cultural traditions and environment. As such, age old cultural festivals are being preserved and promoted to this day. Bhutan also has approximately 72% of the total land area under forest cover with ten national parks and santuaries.

This has helped Bhutan project itself as a tourist destination. People visit Bhutan to experience its unique tradition and culture, to see its intact environment and rich biodiversity, to trek along the Himalayan ranges and to participate in river rafting and moutain biking.

Entry to Bhutan is easy. Two airlines, Drukair and Bhutan Airlines, fly to and from Bangkok, Singapore, New Delhi, Kolkata and Kathmandu. Entry by road is also possible from the Indian state of West Bengal and Assam. Within the country, road networks connect all regions. Bhutan also has three domestic airports in operation.
